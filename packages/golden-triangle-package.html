About This Package

The Golden Triangle is India's most iconic road trip — connecting three of the country's most historically rich cities: Delhi, Agra, and Jaipur. This circuit covers three UNESCO World Heritage Sites, centuries of Mughal and Rajput history, and an extraordinary diversity of food, culture, and architecture — all in one seamless journey.

With your personal driver navigating the expressways and state highways, you have the freedom to linger at the Taj Mahal at sunrise, explore Agra Fort at your own pace, discover the lanes of Jaipur's walled city, and make impromptu stops at roadside dhabas and viewpoints. Our drivers know the best entry timings, parking spots, and local secrets on this route.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

01
Day 1
Delhi — Agra (via Yamuna Expressway)
Depart Delhi early morning to beat traffic. Drive down the smooth Yamuna Expressway (210 km, ~3 hrs). Arrive Agra and head straight to the Taj Mahal for the golden morning light. Explore Agra Fort in the afternoon — a massive red sandstone fortress with stunning views of the Taj across the river. Evening stroll in the Taj Ganj market area. Overnight in Agra.
Taj Mahal Agra Fort Mehtab Bagh
02
Day 2
Agra — Fatehpur Sikri — Jaipur
After breakfast, visit the ghost city of Fatehpur Sikri — Akbar's deserted capital, built entirely in red sandstone. A 45-minute detour that feels like stepping back 500 years. Continue drive to Jaipur (230 km, ~4 hrs). Check in to your hotel in the Pink City. Evening walk along Johari Bazaar for gems and Rajasthani handicrafts.
Fatehpur Sikri Jaipur Johari Bazaar
03
Day 3
Jaipur Sightseeing — Amber Fort, City Palace, Hawa Mahal
Full day Jaipur sightseeing. Start with the magnificent Amber Fort — ride an elephant or jeep up to this hilltop palace. Visit the mirrored Sheesh Mahal inside. Drive past Jal Mahal (the lake palace) on the way back. Afternoon at City Palace complex and the famous Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds). Optional sunset visit to Nahargarh Fort for panoramic Jaipur views.
Amber Fort City Palace Hawa Mahal
04
Day 4
Jaipur — Delhi (Return)
Morning at leisure in Jaipur — last minute shopping at Bapu Bazaar for Rajasthani textiles, blue pottery, and spices. Depart for Delhi (270 km via NH48, ~4.5 hrs). Optional en-route stop at Neemrana Fort Palace for photos and chai. Drop-off at your preferred Delhi location.
Bapu Bazaar Neemrana (optional) Delhi Drop

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to do the Golden Triangle tour?
October to March is ideal — cool, pleasant weather for sightseeing. Avoid peak summer (April–June) when temperatures in Agra and Jaipur can exceed 45°C. The monsoon (July–September) can be done but some roads may be affected.
Is the Taj Mahal closed on any day?
Yes — the Taj Mahal is closed every Friday for regular visitors. Plan your Agra visit accordingly. Our drivers and team will inform you of the best entry slots and help you avoid long queues, especially at sunrise.
Is the price per person or per car?
All our prices are per car, not per person. Whether you're 2 or 7 people in the car, the price remains the same — making it very economical for families and groups.
Can we skip Fatehpur Sikri and go straight to Jaipur?
Absolutely. Your itinerary is completely flexible. You can skip any stop, add extra time at a site you love, or include additional destinations like Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary or Mathura/Vrindavan. Just tell us while booking.
Can we extend the tour to include more cities?
Yes. Popular extensions include Ranthambore National Park (from Jaipur), Pushkar (Ajmer), Udaipur, and Varanasi. We can plan a full Rajasthan circuit or North India mega-tour based on your interests and available days.
