About This Package

The Spiti Valley expedition is India's ultimate off-road adventure — a journey into a high-altitude cold desert that feels like a different planet. Flanked by barren moonscapes, ancient Buddhist monasteries perched impossibly on clifftops, and glacial rivers of turquoise, Spiti is North India's best-kept secret. Your experienced driver will handle the rough mountain tracks so you can simply absorb the raw, untouched beauty.

Accessible from Shimla (via Kinnaur, open most of the year) or Manali (via Rohtang Pass, open June–October), our drivers know both routes intimately. We recommend the Manali–Spiti–Shimla circuit for the complete experience, driving one route up and the other down.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

01
Day 1
Delhi — Shimla
Depart Delhi early morning. Drive via NH48 and NH5 to Shimla (350 km). Stop at Chandigarh for breakfast. Arrive Shimla by afternoon — stroll the famous Mall Road and enjoy views of the surrounding pine forests. Overnight halt at Shimla.
Delhi Chandigarh Shimla
02
Day 2
Shimla — Kinnaur — Sangla Valley
Drive through the stunning Kinnaur district alongside the Sutlej River. Pass the famous Hindustan-Tibet Road, stop at the dramatic Chhitkul village — the last inhabited village near the India-China border. Overnight in Sangla Valley amid apple orchards.
Kinnaur Valley Chhitkul Sangla
03
Day 3
Sangla — Tabo — Kaza
Enter the trans-Himalayan cold desert of Spiti. Drive past the ancient Tabo Monastery (975 AD) — a UNESCO heritage site. Stop at Dhankar Fort perched dramatically over the confluence of Pin and Spiti rivers. Arrive at Kaza, the headquarters of Spiti Valley.
Tabo Monastery Dhankar Fort Kaza
04
Day 4
Kaza Sightseeing — Key Monastery, Kibber, Chicham Bridge
Full day exploring the Kaza area. Visit Key Monastery (1000+ years old) on a dramatic hilltop. Drive to Kibber — one of the world's highest motorable villages at 4,270m. Walk across Asia's highest suspension bridge at Chicham, connecting two villages across a 150m gorge. Return to Kaza.
Key Monastery Kibber Village Chicham Bridge
05
Day 5
Kaza — Pin Valley — Chandratal Lake
Drive into Pin Valley National Park — habitat of the rare snow leopard. Village homestays and yak caravans make this feel like stepping back in time. Continue to the legendary Chandratal (Moon Lake) at 4,300m — a crescent-shaped glacial lake of impossible blue. Camp overnight near the lake.
Pin Valley Chandratal Lake
06
Day 6
Chandratal — Manali (Return via Rohtang Pass)
Early morning watch the sunrise paint Chandratal in golden hues. Drive over the Kunzum Pass (4,590m) and descend through Lahaul Valley to Manali via the Rohtang Pass (permit required). The contrast from the moonscape of Spiti to the green Kullu Valley is breathtaking. Overnight Manali.
Kunzum Pass Rohtang Pass Manali
07
Day 7
Manali — Delhi (Return)
Early morning departure from Manali. Drive back to Delhi via NH3 through the Kullu Valley, Mandi, Kiratpur and Chandigarh. Arriving in Delhi by late evening or next morning. Drop-off at your preferred location.
Kullu Valley Delhi Drop

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Spiti Valley?
June to September is the best window when both Rohtang Pass and Kunzum Pass are open. For the Shimla–Kinnaur route (which avoids Rohtang), travel is possible from April to November. Winter (October–May) via Manali is not recommended due to heavy snowfall and road closures.
Is Spiti safe for first-time mountain travellers?
Yes, with an experienced driver and a capable vehicle. Our drivers have done this route multiple times and know the safe driving protocols for high altitude, narrow roads, and river crossings. We also recommend acclimatisation stops to avoid altitude sickness.
Is the price per person or per car?
All our prices are per car, not per person. Whether you're 2 or 6 people in the car, the price remains the same — making it extremely economical for groups and families travelling together.
Is there mobile network connectivity in Spiti?
Mobile connectivity is limited and mostly available only in Kaza (BSNL works best). Most of Spiti is off the grid — which is part of the charm. We recommend downloading offline maps before the trip. Our drivers carry satellite communication for emergencies.
Can we customize the Spiti itinerary?
Absolutely. You can add Langza village (fossils and Buddha statue), Komic village, Hikkim (world's highest post office), or Losar. You can also extend the trip towards Leh. Just let us know while booking and we'll plan accordingly.
