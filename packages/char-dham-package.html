About This Package

The Char Dham Yatra — covering Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath base and Badrinath — is one of the most sacred pilgrimages in Hinduism. Set amidst the breathtaking Garhwal Himalayas in Uttarakhand, this circuit holds deep spiritual significance and draws lakhs of devotees every year from May to November.

Our experienced drivers have completed this route many times and understand the terrain, road conditions, and the specific requirements of a pilgrimage journey. We ensure a comfortable, safe and spiritually enriching road trip so you can focus on your prayers and the divine experience.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

01
Day 1
Delhi — Haridwar / Rishikesh
Depart Delhi early morning. Drive via NH58 to Haridwar (230 km). Visit Har Ki Pauri Ghat, attend the evening Ganga Aarti if time permits. Overnight stay at Haridwar or Rishikesh — the gateway to the Char Dham Yatra.
Delhi Haridwar Rishikesh
02
Day 2
Haridwar — Barkot (Yamunotri Base)
Drive from Haridwar to Barkot (210 km) via Dehradun and Mussoorie. The road winds through dense forests and mountain valleys. Barkot serves as the base camp for Yamunotri Dham. Overnight stay at Barkot.
Dehradun Mussoorie Barkot
03
Day 3
Yamunotri Dham Darshan
Drive to Janki Chatti (30 km), then trek 6 km to Yamunotri Temple — the source of the sacred Yamuna River. Take a dip in the Surya Kund hot spring, offer prayers, and return to Barkot by evening.
Janki Chatti Yamunotri Temple
04
Day 4
Barkot — Uttarkashi — Gangotri
Drive from Barkot to Gangotri (170 km) via Uttarkashi along the Bhagirathi River. The route is scenic with gorges, rapids and temples. Reach Gangotri, attend evening Aarti and spend the night at altitude near the Gangotri Temple.
Uttarkashi Gangotri
05
Day 5
Gangotri Darshan — Sitapur / Guptkashi
Morning darshan at Gangotri Temple — origin of the Ganges. Drive toward Kedarnath base via Sitapur or Guptkashi (220 km). Panoramic views of the Garhwal Himalayan peaks. Overnight halt at Guptkashi or Phata.
Gangotri Temple Guptkashi
06
Day 6
Kedarnath Base — Gaurikund — Kedarnath Trek
Drive to Gaurikund (55 km). Trek 16 km uphill to Kedarnath Temple (3,583 m altitude). One of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva, Kedarnath sits amidst snow-covered peaks. Pony and doli services available. Overnight at Kedarnath or return to base.
Gaurikund Kedarnath Temple
07–08
Day 7–8
Kedarnath — Badrinath Dham
Return from Kedarnath trek, drive via Joshimath to Badrinath (220 km). Pass through Chopta, Ukhimath and Pipalkoti. Arrive at Badrinath — one of the Char Dham and a Divya Desam of Vishnu. Evening Aarti at the sacred Badrinath Temple.
Joshimath Badrinath
09–10
Day 9–10
Badrinath — Haridwar — Delhi (Return)
Morning darshan at Badrinath Temple. Begin the return journey via Joshimath and Rishikesh back to Haridwar and Delhi. Your driver will plan rest stops at scenic halts. Drop-off at your preferred Delhi location by late evening.
Rishikesh Haridwar Delhi Drop

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time for Char Dham Yatra?
The Char Dham temples open in May (Akshaya Tritiya) and close in November (Diwali / Bhai Dooj). The best months are May–June (before monsoon) and September–October (after monsoon, clearer skies). Avoid peak July–August monsoon if possible due to landslide risk.
Can elderly or senior citizens do this yatra comfortably?
Yes. Our drivers are experienced with senior pilgrim groups. For Kedarnath, ponies, dolis (palanquins) and helicopter services are available at additional cost. Yamunotri and Gangotri have manageable treks with pony options too.
Is the price per person or per car?
All prices are per car, not per person. A group of 6–7 in an Innova Crysta makes this very cost-effective per person compared to tour buses. The more people in the car, the cheaper it gets per head.
Do you help with accommodation bookings along the route?
We primarily provide car with driver service. However, we can recommend reliable hotels, dharamshalas and GMVN guesthouses along the Char Dham route. Booking in advance is strongly recommended especially in peak season (May–June).
Can we customize the number of days?
Absolutely. Some groups do the Char Dham in 12–14 days with a more relaxed pace and additional stops like Auli, Chopta or Tungnath. Others complete it in 10 days at a faster pace. We plan according to your preference.
