About This Package

Jim Corbett National Park — India's oldest and most celebrated national park — lies just 260 km from Delhi, making it the perfect short escape for wildlife lovers. Established in 1936, Corbett is home to Bengal tigers, leopards, elephants, sloth bears, and over 600 species of birds. The park's dense sal forests, Ramganga riverbanks, and varied terrain make every safari a unique adventure.

With your personal car and driver, you travel in comfort from Delhi, avoid crowded buses and shared jeeps, and arrive at your resort refreshed. Our drivers are familiar with the best resort areas, park entry gates, and safari operators — saving you time and hassle so you can focus on spotting the big cats.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

01
Day 1
Delhi — Ramnagar (Jim Corbett)
Depart Delhi early morning (5–6 AM). Drive via NH9 through Gajraula and Moradabad, reaching the Corbett buffer zone by late morning (~5 hrs, 260 km). En route, stop at Garjia Devi Temple — a beautiful riverside temple perched on a large rock in the Kosi river, just 15 km from Ramnagar. Check in to your jungle resort. Evening nature walk around the resort buffer zone. Briefing about next day's safari.
Delhi Garjia Temple Ramnagar
02
Day 2
Corbett National Park Safari — Bijrani / Dhikala Zone
Early morning wake-up for the sunrise jeep safari (6–9 AM) — the best time for wildlife sightings. Enter Bijrani or Dhikala zone (zone allocated by forest department). Track Bengal tigers, leopards, elephants, deer, and exotic birds along the Ramganga riverbanks and sal forests. Afternoon rest. Optional second evening safari (3–6 PM). Visit Corbett Falls (25 km from Ramnagar) in the afternoon — a beautiful cascade through the jungle.
Bijrani Zone Ramganga River Corbett Falls
03
Day 3
Corbett — Delhi (Return)
Optional early morning bird-watching walk before checkout. After breakfast, depart for Delhi. Drive back via the same scenic Terai forest route. Optional stop at Ramnagar market for souvenirs. Arrive Delhi by evening. Drop-off at your preferred location.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Jim Corbett?
November to June is the best time. The park is closed during monsoon (July–October) for conservation. March to June offers the highest chance of tiger sightings as animals gather near water sources. Winter (November–February) has excellent birding and pleasant weather.
Do you arrange the jungle safari booking too?
Our package covers your car and driver from Delhi to Corbett. We can connect you with trusted safari operators and resorts, but the jeep safari and park entry permits need to be booked separately as they require government allocation. We're happy to guide you through the process.
Is the price per person or per car?
All our prices are per car, not per person. Whether you are 2 or 7 people sharing the car, the price stays the same — making it ideal for families and small groups.
Which zone is best for tiger sightings?
Dhikala zone is considered the best for tiger and elephant sightings — it's the core zone of the park with diverse habitat. Bijrani and Jhirna zones are also very good and more accessible. Zone allocation is handled by the forest department at the time of booking safaris.
Can we extend the trip to Nainital or Rishikesh?
Absolutely. Nainital is just 65 km from Ramnagar and Rishikesh is 170 km away — easy add-ons for a longer Uttarakhand circuit. Haridwar can also be combined. Call us and we'll plan the extended itinerary with adjusted pricing.
